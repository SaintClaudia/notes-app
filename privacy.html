Privacy Policy

Last updated July 10, 2026

Modo is built to be fast and private. This page explains, plainly, what data the app touches and where it goes.

What's stored, and where

Your notes — titles, tags, and content — are stored locally on your device. No account or sign-in is required to use the app.

On iOS and iPadOS, if you're signed into iCloud, notes also sync across your own devices via Apple's CloudKit, into your private iCloud database. That data is governed by your Apple ID and Apple's own privacy practices — we (the developer) have no access to it. Sync can be turned off at any time by signing out of iCloud on your device.

The web version at notesforyou.app stores notes only in your browser's local storage. It is not connected to iCloud sync.

AI dashboard summaries

The dashboard shows a short summary of your active notes. By default this is generated entirely on your device. If a network connection is available, the app may also request an AI-generated version of that same summary from our backend, which forwards the request to Anthropic's Claude API.

When this happens:

  • Notes marked private, archived, or empty are never included in that request.
  • Only note titles, tags, and text content needed to write the summary are sent — no device identifiers tied to your identity, no location, no contacts.
  • A random identifier generated on your device is sent alongside the request solely to apply a rate limit (capping how many requests one device can make per hour) and is not linked to your name, email, or Apple ID.
  • If the request fails or you're offline, the app silently falls back to the on-device summary — nothing is retried or queued.

What we don't do

  • No analytics, ad tracking, or third-party SDKs.
  • No accounts, no passwords, no email collection.
  • No selling or sharing of your data with anyone beyond what's described above.

Children's privacy

Modo is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date at the top of this page will change accordingly.
